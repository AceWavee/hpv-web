Understanding HPV

What is HPV?

Human Papillomavirus (HPV) is a group of over 200 related viruses. It's the most common sexually transmitted infection, affecting both men and women.

HPV Types

High-risk types (16, 18, 31, 33, 45, 52, 58): Can cause cancers
Low-risk types (6, 11): Cause genital warts
Types 16 & 18 cause 70% of cervical cancers

How it Spreads

HPV spreads through skin-to-skin contact during sexual activity. It can affect the genital area, throat, and other areas of the body.

Who's at Risk

Anyone who is sexually active can get HPV. Risk factors include multiple partners, early sexual activity, weakened immune system, and smoking.

Prevention Methods

HPV Vaccination Schedule

Ages 11-12

2 doses - 6-12 months apart
Routine recommendation

Ages 15+

3 doses - 0, 1-2, 6 months
Catch-up vaccination

Ages 27-45

3 doses - 0, 1-2, 6 months
Shared decision with doctor

HPV Vaccination

90%+ Effective

Gardasil 9 vaccine protects against 9 HPV types. Most effective when given before exposure.

Regular Screening

Early Detection

Pap tests and HPV tests every 3-5 years depending on age and test type.

Safe Sex Practices

Partial Protection

Condom use reduces but doesn't eliminate risk as HPV affects areas not covered.

Limiting Partners

Risk Reduction

Reducing number of sexual partners can help lower HPV transmission risk.

Screening Guidelines

Ages 21-29

Pap test every 3 years

Ages 30-65

HPV test every 5 years (preferred) or Pap + HPV test every 5 years

Ages 65+

No screening if adequate prior screening and not high-risk
